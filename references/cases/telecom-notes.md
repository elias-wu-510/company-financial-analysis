# Telecom Case Notes

Use this note for telecom operators, tower businesses, broadband platforms, converged connectivity providers, and other network-heavy communications businesses.

## Why telecom is easy to misread

Telecom companies often report large revenue bases and EBITDA, but the real questions are usually about:
- subscriber quality versus headline subscriber count
- ARPU and mix quality
- capex intensity and network economics
- churn, pricing discipline, and competitive structure

## Common pitfalls

### 1. Treating subscriber growth as enough

Subscriber growth alone can hide:
- weaker ARPU
n- lower-quality prepaid mix
- promotional pressure
- bundled-product dilution

### 2. Treating EBITDA as full proof of business quality

Telecom is capital intensive. EBITDA can look strong while:
- depreciation remains heavy
- spectrum or network investment stays elevated
- free cash flow remains constrained

### 3. Ignoring network-investment timing

A weak free-cash-flow period may reflect:
- 5G rollout
- fibre build-out
- backbone / data-centre expansion
- spectrum payments

Do not label this automatically as business deterioration without context.

### 4. Missing segment economics

Mobile, fixed broadband, enterprise, wholesale, tower, and digital services often have different economics.

Do not collapse them into one story without explaining mix effects.

## What a good telecom report should explain

- whether growth comes from users, pricing, mix, or enterprise contracts
- whether EBITDA strength is converting into cash after network investment
- whether leverage is manageable given spectrum and capex needs
- whether the business model depends on pricing discipline, bundling, or infrastructure ownership

## Useful framing questions

- Is the operator winning on scale, premium positioning, network quality, or low-price acquisition?
- Is the company monetising data growth efficiently or just carrying traffic growth with weak returns?
- Does the balance sheet support the next investment cycle without overstretch?
