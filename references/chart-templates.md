# Chart Templates

Use this reference when producing deliverable-quality company-analysis charts. Prefer a small number of clear charts over many overlapping charts.

## Goal

Give the report reusable chart patterns that answer common financial-analysis questions without falling into comparability traps.

## Template 1 — Revenue Mix Block

### Question it answers
What does the company actually earn from, and how did business mix change?

### Best use cases
- segment revenue mix
- domestic vs overseas mix
- recurring vs cyclical revenue mix

### Recommended format
- stacked bar for one period or two like-for-like periods
- one unit only
- one period type only

### Guardrails
- do not mix parent and child segments in one stack
- do not compare FY and H1 in the same revenue-mix chart as if directly comparable
- if classifications changed, explain the bridge before charting

## Template 2 — Recurring vs Non-Recurring Bridge

### Question it answers
How much of reported profit comes from recurring operations versus timing-sensitive or one-off items?

### Best use cases
- property-linked companies
- infrastructure platforms with development or disposal gains
- companies with revaluation or other non-operating swings

### Recommended format
- bridge or split table/chart
- clearly separate:
  - recurring operating contribution
  - development / disposal / valuation contribution
  - reported total

### Guardrails
- do not imply that a large one-off contribution improves recurring quality
- label each contribution clearly as recurring or non-recurring

## Template 3 — Leverage and Liquidity Panel

### Question it answers
Is the balance sheet improving because of real deleveraging, stronger liquidity, equity growth, or hybrid-capital effects?

### Best use cases
- net debt-to-equity
- gross debt
- cash balance
- undrawn facilities
- interest cover

### Recommended format
Use a compact panel with 3-4 small exhibits:
- leverage ratio trend
- debt vs cash snapshot
- liquidity buffer note
- financing interpretation note

### Guardrails
- do not show a lower leverage ratio without explaining whether debt fell, cash rose, equity rose, or hybrid capital changed the denominator
- keep point-in-time balance-sheet metrics separate from period earnings metrics unless clearly labeled as context only

## Template 4 — Margin / Driver Pair

### Question it answers
Why did profit or margin change?

### Best use cases
- transport operations
- utilities
- telecom
- any segment where costs and mix drive interpretation

### Recommended format
Pair:
- one chart/table showing margin or EBIT/EBITDA change
- one evidence block naming the top 1-3 disclosed drivers

### Guardrails
- do not stop at "costs increased"
- use the most specific driver disclosed
- if certainty is partial, state that clearly

## Template 5 — Peer Framing Snapshot

### Question it answers
How does the target company’s business model differ from peers?

### Best use cases
- operating-model comparison
- capital-structure comparison
- recurring-income model comparison

### Recommended format
Use a compact comparison table, not a decorative chart, unless peer metrics are truly like-for-like.

### Guardrails
- do not compare mixed FY / H1 / TTM data without a warning
- do not rank peers with non-comparable metric definitions
- interpret the differences instead of dumping numbers only

## Delivery rule

A chart template is useful only if:
- the periods are clear
- the metrics are comparable
- the question is specific
- the note explains what the reader should take away
