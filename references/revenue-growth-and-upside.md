# Revenue Growth and Future Upside

Use this reference when analysing a company where readers care not only about current revenue, but also about where future growth may come from.

## Default emphasis

Revenue growth and future upside should be treated as a default focus area in company analysis unless the user clearly asks for a narrower accounting-only review.

## What to explain

### 1. Current revenue growth
- what revenue changed
- which segment changed
- whether growth came from volume, pricing, mix, project contribution, recovery, or one-off factors

### 2. Revenue quality
- whether the growth is recurring or timing-sensitive
- whether the growth is broad-based or concentrated
- whether it depends on asset disposals, development timing, or policy support

### 3. Future growth upside
- what projects, capacity additions, openings, expansions, or strategic initiatives may support future growth
- whether those drivers are already visible in the reported period or mainly future pipeline
- what could prevent that upside from materialising

## Delivery rule

A strong company-analysis report should not stop at stating current revenue. It should also explain where future growth may come from, how credible that growth path is, and what risks could weaken it.
